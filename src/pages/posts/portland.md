I really enjoyed writing about fear in my last blog post because now I can finally get to the juicy parts of why I even wrote that simple little thought I had in my head down on my infamous blog. I drove 2,600 miles from Atlanta, Georgia all the way to Portland, Oregon in FIVE days. Yes, I drove across the entire country to a new city. Whereas I have done this in the past like in Miami & a small town in Texas, I didn't stay for a week or half a year. I plan to live, work & die out in the PNW. As I have been huddled in my abuser's house for far too long, I decided that enough was enough and it's time for me to start actually living MY life. 

I have had zero interest in staying in Atlanta for a lot of reasons, one being I had the ability to leave. The second reason being that I wanted to finally live somewhere where I can actually be myself. If everyone is weird, no one is. I love that so much. It's also beautiful, but I will go into more detail in the later portions of this blog post. Which I will do so now.

## Day 1

I have been thinking about moving to the PNW since I was in my early 20s. Portlandia came out and I was like, if I was going to live anywhere, it was going to be there. Fred Armisen, thank you! I was living with my abuser prior to the move and he would not pay for AC, so I paid for my portable AC. However, as soon as it broke, I said fuck it and packed whatever I could. From henceforth, my life was in my hands as I put my coordinates in for Portland into my phone and drove off. Don't worry, I wasn't traveling alone, I had my companion Mina along for the ride to keep me company on the road.

I knew the journey was going to be long & arduous so I told myself multiple times to take it easy. Driving through North Georgia was a piece of cake. I'm glad I no longer have to drive into Atlanta anymore, God that was such a nightmare. I remember when I was staying at an apartment downtown to go to Georgia State and there was a Racetrack where students would have to be careful. Safe to say, one of the students didn't make it and rest his soul for what a tragedy Downtown Atlanta and GSU has handled anything that isn't donations. Anyways, I got through Georgia into Tennessee. Tennessee was a pretty smooth drive through some mountains but somewhere along the road it became like driving through Atlanta and I was like "Oh no, I hope this isn't most of the ride over." Understand, I was also a bit sleep deprived so I was driving like a grandma through the traffic.

Once I got through Tennessee to Kentucky it was a cakewalk, I took myself a bathroom break at one of those rest stops and smooth sailing to whatever state I could get to next. Kentucky was a very boring state but I really liked that. If the roads weren't complete ass I could see myself coming back. Too bad there is nothing that really piques my interest in that state. After a couple hours I finally got to Illinois. I was very happy with myself so I found a rest stop and called it for tonight. 

## Day 2
I was pretty sleep deprived so getting some sleep and a nice hearty breakfast from the place I was staying at was just what I needed from this trip. My goal for Day 2 was to get to Lincoln, Nebraska. One thing I do want to note is that I did see a lot of construction projects going on throughout this journey. Good to see people fixing the roads but it did take away parts of the trip for me. Illinois was the worst of it all but after getting into Missouri I was like it's okay. You can tell St. Louis ain't what it used to be. From there I drove all the way towards Kansas City. I was listening to an audiobook from YouTube at this time because I didn't want to die from boredom. 

Day 2 was pretty boring. Franz Kafka's Metamorphosis was just what the doctor ordered. It was a great listen and had me realizing that people-pleasing is bug behavior and no one will respect you if you don't respect yourself. Books truly are the best ever. After listening to it I realized that I should have listened to way more on the trip.

I finally got like an hour outside of Lincoln and stayed in a shady motel. You know a place is shady when they have a casino inside the place. It was shitty but I needed somewhere to stay and thankfully nothing was stolen. Also, I just want to say that there was nothing very exciting about the middle of the country. Iowa, Nebraska & Missouri are not states you want to visit. 

## Day 3

Got out as soon as possible and hit the road. Thankfully, nothing was stolen or broken into. It was time for me to drive all the way from Nebraska to Wyoming. Funny because an entire day's drive was just in two states. I need to stop talking about Nebraska but I will say that in elementary school I made up a story to one of my teachers that I knew a girl from Nebraska who played the violin. That's about all I can say about that state. 

However, this is where the trip got entertaining. Entering Wyoming was super exciting because I knew that I was no longer on the eastern portion of the US. I also slowly realized that there really was no turning back and that I was on my own. Can I just say how gorgeous this state is though. You never hear anyone talk about it but this state was beautiful. The windmills, the mountains, the hills. I took a rest stop here and saw Lincoln's head. I wish I took pictures but it's worth going out to Wyoming to visit.

An hour before I hit my resting place, I grabbed some food at an Indian restaurant since I haven't been to one before. Telling them I didn't want the "spice", I could tell they were snickering but that didn't bother me because I was on an adventure and I NEED to get out of my comfort zone. Got some curry, rice & shrimp. YUMMY! Time to head out.

Finally got near the end of the border around 6pm mountain time and went into a very nice hotel this time. Rock Springs is where I finally hit the hay and for Day 4, I set my sights on Boise, Idaho. Closing in on Portland ever so slightly.

## Day 4

Made myself a very nice breakfast burrito, some coffee & off I went. My goal for this part of the trip was to hit Boise, Idaho. Once again, Wyoming was a beautiful state. It's a blessing for some that it's the least populated state, personally, I love that for them. Let's keep Wyoming peaceful. After about a couple hours on the road I hit Utah. Two more states to go. Utah was also very beautiful for like an hour or so then it looked like Mars. Not a lot to say about Utah. I did hit one gas station on the way from Utah and Idaho. It was crappy and people were rushing me so I got on the road as soon as possible. After that, I finally made it to Idaho.

I just want to preface this by saying that I thought Nebraska was the worst state on my drive here. I'm currently laughing my ass off. Idaho fucking blows. Whereas you are driving up mountainous roads, Idaho wants you to fall & die. There are no guardrails anywhere and driving through this state was also like driving through the conservative backwaters except it's on the west. Massive men with massive egos with massive trucks driving 20 over the speed limit and my ass is trying not to die all the while. Also, might be the ugliest state I've ever been to as well. Looked like I was on the planet Venus most of the way over until I hit Boise.

Then when I hit Boise it reminded me a lot of when I hit Huntsville Alabama. A liberal place in the middle of a giant conservative state. I got some food at Panera and I was like, yeah, I am definitely on the west coast. I'm not sure how to best describe it other than vibes. Finally got a place to stay in the suburbs and rested. Tomorrow was the final day. I was exhausted but excited nonetheless. 

## Day 5

Finally, I only had one state to go before I finally got to Portland. I'll be honest, it's been a month since I've been out here and I fricking love it. However, there was always a place in the back of my mind where I was questioning whether it would even work out here. Thankfully, it did but that was a creeping feeling that I hid away and drove 500 more miles to Portland.

As soon as I got to Oregon, my trusted traveling companion Mina and I yelled & screamed for joy. The start of a new chapter in my life has begun. The realization slowly hit me and I started losing it, in a good way. I bawled my eyes out while driving under the speed limit. I am alone. I am happy. I can finally start living MY life and not OTHER people's lives. This is my life.

After all those feelings came over me, I still had to reach Portland. I was also very tired so I drove pretty relaxed most of the way through. I also thought Wyoming was the most beautiful state. Driving from the border of Idaho/Oregon to Portland is a sight to behold. Mountains, rivers & the whole for the eye to see. It was one of the best car rides I have ever experienced in my life. It was nirvana. 

I got to a couple of rest stops, one was at the tippy top of the mountains which was a nice vibe before I hit the one where you can see Washington state across the river. I looked out and said to myself, "this is my home".

## Summary

In conclusion, "Every man has two lives and the second starts when he realizes he has just one" – Confucius. If you want to do something, do it. I wanted to move to Portland and I have never felt more alive in my entire life than I have been out here. Working on finding a job & meeting as many people has been a fever dream and I don't want to wake up. Make fear your passenger princess.

By the way! Shoutout to Mina, I wouldn't have been able to do it without you being with me, thank you.
