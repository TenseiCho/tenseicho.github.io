## 3 "Easy" Steps

As someone who has been at the lowest point in my life, it's been nice to finally build myself back up and see where this new road takes me. I figured
my 1st blog post should be about how to fix your life since this is where I am at in my journey in life.

## Step 1: Overcome your trauma

I believe that you will never be happy and content with yourself if you can never complete this step. Not being able to do this step will lead you to complete darkness if you never figure out what your trauma is and overcome it. Notice how I don't say "fix it", because that would imply you can repair an already broken foundation. 

No plans of mine to go into detail about what it was that caused my old problems but I am nonetheless happy to finally be able to focus on the more important aspects of life. Some people I know may never truly ever complete this Step in life and wonder why they can't fix certain issues in their life. The onus is not on you to fix their issues, it's on you to be the best version of yourself possible. 


## Step 2: Identify your problems

Now, this is where the rubber meets the road. You've got to take a good, hard look at your life and figure out what's not working. No sugar-coating, no excuses. Just honest assessment.
Here's what that looked like for me:

- I am morbidly obese. Not just "could lose a few pounds," but full-on, health-at-risk obese. This wasn't about aesthetics - it was about being able to live the life I wanted. Going outside and truly living a life I could be proud of.

- I need friends who are aligned with my values. Your social circle has a bigger impact on your life than you might think. I realized I needed to surround myself with people who would support and inspire me, not lie to my face & backstab me.

- I don't have a job. Not just "between gigs," but properly unemployed. This was affecting not just my relationship with my friends, but my sense of purpose and self-worth.

Your list might look different. Maybe you're drowning in debt, or your relationships are on life support. Whatever your problems are, the key is to identify them clearly and honestly. You can't fix what you won't acknowledge.

## Step 3: ?????

This is the fun part. It's on you now to fix it. And guess what? I'm right there in the trenches with you, doing it right now.

For me, this step has been all about making small, consistent changes that add up to big results. Here's what it looks like:

- Working out every day: But not in the way you might think. I've found activities I actually enjoy, like Beat Saber (who knew flailing your arms to music could be exercise?) and walking. Turns out, moving your body can have beneficial rather than deleterious effects.

- Changing my gaming habits: I've stepped away from the black hole that is MOBA games like Dota. Sorry, dotards, you'll have to find someone else to carry you.

- Choosing my company wisely: I'm being nice to people, but I'm also not afraid to ignore those who don't align with my values. It's not being mean, it's called self-preservation.

- Checking my ego at the door: I'm learning that my ego isn't always my amigo. Sometimes, it's the very thing holding me back.

- Stepping out of my comfort zone: I'm meeting people outside, trying new things like pickleball (it's a real sport, I swear), and getting involved in my church community.

- Working on projects: Like this very blog post you're reading. It's not just about filling time; it's about creating something meaningful.

Here's the kicker: each of these steps doesn't just improve one area of my life - they have a domino effect. Losing weight makes me feel better about going outside to meet new people. Meeting new people opens up new employment opportunities. 

Your Step 3 will be different. I wish you the best of luck and thank you for reading my very first blog in the long line of posts to come. 

Ganbatte!
