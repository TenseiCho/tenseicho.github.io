I’m sure we can all laugh at ourselves. Myself included, obviously. The funniest thing about all of this was the idea that driving twenty-six hundred miles across the country wasn’t going to be as painful as writing out that last blogpost. Driving twenty-six hundred miles across the country was a heck of a lot more painful than writing an apology letter to a dear friend of mine. It's been a while since I had a flow state like that. Writing about how shitty of a person I was to them made me realize a lot of things. If they never humbled me, I don’t want to imagine, actually. While I was thinking on that point, I started writing again because I realized how fallible of a person I am. Even if I mean well, some things can get jumbled in the process. I’m keenly aware of that now. Maybe. Time will tell.

I believe that I may or may not have come across as a victim in that last post. I just want to set the record straight. I wasn’t the victim—I was the perpetrator. It’s easy to blame the people around you until you finally look deep inside, stare at yourself and start to see some reflections.

## You reap what you sow 

After reading that last blog post over & over again. I realized that it’s kind of ass. I was under the impression it was my magnum opus. I must have been huffing galaxy gas. It actually frightened me. Not the noxious fumes, but the fact that I ignored good advice for so long. The times people did nudge me in the right direction, I was like “Nah, my unconventional wisdom shall prevail and you, mister know it all, are wrong”. As I say that in the most condescending way imaginable to people that I “love” & “care" about. Disgusting. 

I think realizing that I was the problem all along was what really hurt the most. I guess I was too focused on my previous self that I lost sight of my current self because I was too preoccupied with my future self. Even now, bad habits are starting to come back. I'm spending money I don't have and consuming too much food again. I shouldn't be eating Five Guys. I need to get a job & find a long-term rental. There is no mommy & daddy to go back to. I’m almost 30 anyway, it’s time to grow up.

The world is not black & white. The person that I was disgusts me. I hate that I hurt so many people who cared about me. I will never understand why I chose to be arrogant. I guess ignorance is bliss? It sure doesn’t feel that way. Why did I make that discord my entire identity? Was I too focused on my previous self that I lost sight of my current self because I was too preoccupied with my future self?



## Moving forward

That means I need to stop writing about my past and focus on the present so I can reap the benefits of my future self. 

I got some good ideas for some 3D models that I want to have on my website. Starting to get into working on reference images inside Blender. I am still in tutorial hell but I'm really liking it. Added some new projects to work on to also add to the website as well. Making my own version of Osu on the browser and having a r/a/dio desktop client are among the new projects I'm super ecstatic to finally show off once I'm like 60% done with them. I also question how I am a YouTuber with no videos, that will be changing in the coming months as well.

Once I get fully set up with everything at the new place, I've got some time to stream myself playing Guilty Gear Strive. I've never been consistent with who I was previously but streaming is the easiest thing to do. I feel like I could excel at entertaining people & playing games. That's what I did for the past ten years so why not do the same in my off time. Maybe even make a little fumo money on the side.

That's one thing that was upsetting to me. I went to a discord IRL meetup recently and people asked me what I did for fun. I had no answer. I was a boring person. I LOATHE being a boring person. This entire month has just been me adjusting to being myself again after all the shit I've been through. Playing Drakengard 3, WEBFISHING, The House in Fata Morgana and also reading manga again. If I'm going to work hard, I also want to play hard as well. 

I got a lot of good stuff on the horizon as I am sending out job apps, working on projects, going to the gym, meeting new people in the area & learning to be myself again. I look forward to what awaits me in the coming months because you know I will be sharing it on my website.

Anyways, time is short & I still got a lot of life left to live.

Ciao
