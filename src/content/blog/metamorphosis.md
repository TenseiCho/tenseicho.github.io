I have written blog posts before, but I feel as if I was writing them for people in my past & not for myself. Burdened by this, I deleted most of them. The only thing you need to know about me is that I have had an extremely esoteric life up to this point. I will be keeping that a more guarded secret going forward. I have no qualms with telling anyone anything about my life, but it's really just better to keep my cognitive security clean.

However, what's the point of being yourself if you're not willing to be yourself to the rest of humanity? This post is more a post of reclaiming my destiny. I have rewritten this entry so many times I have lost count. I think this might be the one that will be uploaded. At the end of the day, I'm writing for myself & not for other people. If I am happy with the material, that's all that matters.

## TenseiCho

For context, when I was driving from Kansas City to Omaha, on my way to Portland from Atlanta, I was listening to an audiobook called Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka. It shocked me because it was really entertaining to listen to how I was perceived by the other side of the audience. Some might call it being shellshocked; I call it a reality check.

I have been living a life of pleasing others for the past ten years, and I realized that I have never once wondered what I wanted to do. I have always been the boot to other people, and getting in my car after my portable AC broke in my abuser's house & driving all the way to Oregon made me realize how in control of my life I really was. I can just do whatever I want. That was cathartic.

Since I've been out here, I've been slowly realizing that I have saved my life. Getting away from that environment made me come to terms with the notion that if I had stayed any longer there, I might be dead. That's not an exaggeration. If you knew the way I used to live, I wonder if you would pity me or step on me.

Where does that leave me? Well, I still got a long way to go before I can stand on my feet & be happy with the person that I know I can be. However, that's the point of the title. My name is now Tensei, which means Rebirth. I am no longer the person that I used to know because I died and have been reborn. This is my second chance at life, and thank God I'm still in my 20s to be able to do so. Like a butterfly (cho) emerges from a cocoon, that is literally me!

I think this is a much better introduction than telling people in my past who I plan to be; I'll just become that person instead.
